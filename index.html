Methodology, data & sources +

What this tool does. It estimates the material-production footprint of a garment by multiplying a per-unit impact factor (kg CO₂e, litres of water, and £ cost — per kg of fibre for fabrics, per m² for leathers) by a typical quantity of material in that product. Comparisons are always made within the same material family, so fabric-per-kg and leather-per-m² figures are never mixed in a single result.

What it deliberately does not do. It is not a certified Life Cycle Assessment. It excludes spinning/weaving energy, dyeing, transport, the consumer-use phase (washing and drying often dominate a garment's real footprint), and end-of-life. Retail price is driven by brand, margin and labour far more than material cost, so the "cost" figure models material cost only. Treat every number as an order-of-magnitude estimate for comparison, not a guarantee.

Why confidence ratings. Much of the published data for novel bio-materials comes from the makers themselves, is run on their best-case formulation, or has no independent LCA at all. Rather than hide that, each material carries a high / medium / low confidence flag.
